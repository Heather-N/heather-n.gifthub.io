Springville, Utah

Population: 32,952

History

Most of Utah was settled by members of the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-Day Saints, and Springville was no different. As a large number settled in what is now Salt Lake City, a small group was sent by Brigham Young to settle in what is now Springville. They arrived on September 18, 1850.

The area had been named Hobble Creek a year earlier when an exploration team lost a pair of iron hobbles from one of their horses. The town steadily grew, however, and the name was changed to Springville as we all know it. On February 13, 1853, the town was officially incorporated.

The Art City

Springville, Utah is the center for the arts within Utah. This gained the city the name of the "Art City." Established in 1936, the Springville Museum of Art became the first art museum in Utah, It holds over 1,200 pieces of art, increasing the strength of the name Art City.

The moniker of Art City is shown throughout the city. The "Welcome to Springville" sign shows off the adopted name. In addition, sculptures are a prominent part of the center of the city. Their displays along main street and center street make Springville a unique place to drive through.
